Beef Burger

16 June 2015

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds ground beef (85 percent lean or less)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Split lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Pickle
  • Mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Heat the grill to medium-high. Divide the meat and gently form it into 4 balls. (Do not overwork the meat.)
  2. Flatten the balls into 3/4 inch-thick patties.
  3. Flatten the top and sides of each patty to ensure that the thickness is the same throughout.
  4. Use your fingers to make a shallow well in the top of each patty. (This will prevent over plumping.)
  5. Season the patties with 1/2 teaspoon each salt and pepper.
  6. Oil the grill grate using a pair of tongs and a paper towel dipped in cooking oil.
  7. Cook the burgers (with the wells facing up) until they release easily from the grill, 4 to 5 minutes for medium.
  8. Flip and cook until an instant-read thermometer inserted in the center registers 68°C, or 4 to 5 minutes for medium. Serve on rolls with the desired toppings.
